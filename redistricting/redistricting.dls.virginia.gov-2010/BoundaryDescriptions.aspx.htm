District Boundary Descriptions


Section 30-264 of the Code of Virginia directs the Division of Legislative Services to prepare written descriptions of congressional, Senatorial, and House of Delegates districts.

The description for each district first quotes the description of the district provided in the Code of Virginia. The Code description lists the whole localities contained in the district and then, for parts of counties and cities included in the district, the precincts or parts of precincts that make up the included part. The named precincts are not the current precincts. Rather, they refer to the precincts in existence on April 1, 2011, and used by the United States Bureau of the Census in the 2010 census population reports and shown on 2010 census maps.*

For each partial county or city in a district, the description next lists each road, stream, or other boundary feature that the district line follows in dividing the locality. The boundary segment features are named or otherwise identified with reference to the maps released by the Census Bureau to accompany the 2010 census population data. If the census map does not identify a feature by name, a supplemental source that does name the feature may also have been used and the source is so noted.

The descriptions are provided for information purposes only pursuant to ยง 30-264. The provisions of the Code of Virginia, including the statistical reports referenced therein and on file with the Clerk of the House of Delegates, are controlling in any legal determination of the district boundary.*

*The language from the statute establishing the new districts is as follows: "Parts of counties and cities listed . . . are defined by reference to the 2010 Census reports for the precincts, parts of precincts, and blocks listed for each . . . district in the Statistical Report on file with the Clerk of the House of Delegates for the Act of Assembly containing the final enactment of this section. Precincts shall be interpreted to refer to those in existence on April 1, 2011, and as reported by the United States Bureau of the Census in the 2010 Census reports provided pursuant to United States Public Law 94-171, notwithstanding subsequent changes made by localities."
