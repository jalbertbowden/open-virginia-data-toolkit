Census 2000 Population Counts--Explanations

The 2000 Precinct Reports show the Census 2000 Total Population and Voting Age Population (population 18 and older) for each locality and precinct in Virginia as shown by the Census maps and data reports.

Please read the following explanations of the precinct lists and racial data columns.

THE PRECINCT NAMES AND CODES
The State Board of Elections reported 2,196 active precincts for 2000; the Census 2000 list shows 2,174 precincts. The difference in these two numbers is explained by three factors: combined precincts, multi-part precincts, and special situations explained below.

In preparing for the 2000 Census, Virginia worked with the Census Bureau to draw Virginia's 2,196 active precinct boundaries on the census maps. However, the Census Bureau will not give a population count for a precinct unless the boundaries of the precinct meet the Bureau's standards for census block boundaries. Generally, a census block or precinct boundary must follow visible, identifiable features. Where the local precinct boundary does not follow census block geography, some adjustment has been made on the census maps.

COMBINED PRECINCTS:
The Census 2000 data and maps include 32 "combined" precincts. A "combined" precinct is shown with a precinct code ending in 99 (or 96 through 99 in localities with more than one combination). Seventy-one of Virginia's precincts have been combined into 32 of these "combined" precincts because no reasonable adjustment could be drawn to follow census block boundaries.

MULTI-PART PRECINCTS:
Fourteen local precincts, as shown by the Census maps and data reports, are comprised of non-contiguous parts. There are 14 two-part precincts and one three-part precinct resulting in 31 precincts shown with Part 1, 2, or 3 as parts of the precinct name and a four-digit precinct code. For example, Jackson Heights Parts 1 and 2 are shown with the State Board code for Jackson Heights (402) in Alleghany County plus a 1 or 2.

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:
In two instances, the Census maps and data show a county composed of two populated parts: Fairfax A (Code 059198) is the Fairfax County jail located in Fairfax City. Montgomery A is Precinct D-3 Part 2 (Code 1214032) and is located in Radford City.

ACTUAL AND "PSEUDO" OR FALSE PRECINCTS.
Approximately 68 percent or 1,479 of the Commonwealth's 2,174 census precincts have boundaries that meet the requirements for census block boundaries. The 2000 Census maps and population tables show these precincts with an asterisk to indicate that precinct is an "actual" precinct and the same as the locality's legal precinct.

Approximately 32 percent or 695 of the precincts have boundaries that do not fully meet these census requirements. A part of the precinct's boundary may follow an invisible line and divide one or more census blocks. In these cases, the Division of Legislative Services worked with the Census Bureau and "adjusted" the precinct line for census purposes to follow the nearest census block line. The 2000 Census maps and population tables will show these pseudo" precincts without the asterisk. Note: These "pseudo" precincts are used only for census purposes and to obtain census statistics for precincts. The precincts used to conduct elections are not legally changed by these technical census-related adjustments. However, state legislative and congressional districts that follow census precinct lines will be following "pseudo" or adjusted census precinct lines.

RACIAL COLUMNS AND CATEGORIES
The Census redistricting file contains 63 distinct racial categories or combinations. To simplify this complex data, the 2000 Precinct Reports collapse the racial data into seven columns adapted from guidance documents issued by the Office of Management and Budget in March 2000 and the Department of Justice in January 2001. The Precinct Reports distribute Hispanic population by race for the 100 percent count and then provide a separate count for Hispanic population broken down by race. The user thus can calculate the non-Hispanic component of race groups.

The columns and their component parts for the Total Population and Voting Age Population tables are as follows:

White -- White alone
Black -- Black or African American alone plus White and Black or African American
Am.Ind -- American Indian and Alaska Native alone plus White and American Indian and Alaska Native
Asian -- Asian alone plus White and Asian
HawPI -- Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander alone plus White and Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander
Other -- Some other race alone plus White and some other race
Multi-race -- All other multiple race categories not included in the above categories

Hispanic data is shown in the Total Population and Voting Age Populations tables as follows:

Hisp. -- Total Hispanic or Latino population
Hisp.White -- White alone minus not Hispanic or Latino white
Hisp.Black -- [Black or African American alone plus White and Black or African American] minus [not Hispanic or Latino Black or African American alone plus not Hispanic or Latino White and Black or African American]
Hisp.Other -- All other Hispanic or Latino population not included in the Hisp.White and Hisp.Black columns.
